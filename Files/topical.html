The interplay between technology and society

Task 1, Module 1.1

In the video, Hanne Cecilie discusses how a mobile phone can be understood as a technical device, a social device, as well as a phenomenon that can tell us something about the values in a society. How can a mobile phone be understood through the lens of your field of study and what do you think are the social implications of it? (From module 1.1)

Through the lens of our field of study, the mobile phone can be seen as a tiny computer. Through this tiny computer we can explore the features that form our daily life, like planning, doing tasks “on the go” and communicating with peers. For instance, in our study field we use learning platforms such as canvas and zoom, which require an internet connection. This makes the mobile phone a device quite suited for the tasks, since it does not tie the user down to a specific physical place, as it can be used from anywhere. However, it does require that the device has service and cellular data such as 4G or 5G, or the use of internet in a café, or other hotspots.

The mobile phone was originally a technical device, but over time it has turned into a device more connected to the social aspect of life. Such as communicating with our friends and families over any distances within a short amount of time. However, this may also impact our ability to be social in real life. This gateway into social paradise, can also lead to isolation. For instance, if you don’t have the tools necessary, internet or a phone, or you decide to not be a part of the change this brings to society. This can for example be seen with elderly people that don’t follow the evolution of the technology around them, and get overly dependent on family, or other means of help, to function in society.

As a result, the mobile phone can be seen as one of our most important devices to our professional and social life.

Task 2, Module 1.3

The Internet has grown into a ubiquitous infrastructure that societies all over the world are increasingly reliant on. To which extent and how does the internet have political consequences? Note down some examples and discuss with your colleagues. (From module 1.3)

In the last few years we have seen that elections all over the world have been influenced by the internet, and have caused consequenses for both political and social aspects. A practical example of this is what we have seen from the western society, during the American presidential election in 2020. Donald Trump both during and after the election used his social platforms to undermine the credibility of the election result.(Troyer, 2020) Which may have resulted in the attack on Capitol Hill 6th of January 2021, were Trump supporters stormed Capitol Hill to stop the formalization of President-elect Joe Biden’s victory.(Wikipedia, 2021) This shows the power behind big political influencers skepticisms towards the government and other means of power.

Seeing it from the eastern society perspective could be the Myanmar coup in February 2021. After the military took over February 1st, access to internet and social media was gradually more restricted, culminating in the complete shutdown of the web on the 2nd of April 2021. This makes it difficult for the civil people of Myanmar to know what’s going on in the country at large, and to actively participate in the demonstrations. The bans on social media, especially Facebook, also deeply affects people’s ability to communicate with one another.(Tostevin et al., 2017) One of many consequences of this is that people outside of Myanmar will get biased information when the people themselves have no means of communicating with us.

To conclude, both examples show how restricted flow of information can influence individuals or as a whole group, whether it’s chosen our forced upon them.
