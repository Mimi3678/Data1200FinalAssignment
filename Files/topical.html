The interplay between technology and society

Task 1, Module 1.1 In the video, Hanne Cecilie discusses how a mobile phone can be understood as a technical device, a social device, as well as a phenomenon that can tell us something about the values in a society. How can a mobile phone be understood through the lens of your field of study and what do you think are the social implications of it? (From module 1.1)

Through the lens of our field of study, the mobile phone can be seen as a tiny computer. Through this tiny computer we can explore the features that form our daily life, like planning, doing tasks “on the go” and communicating with peers. For instance, in our study field we use learning platforms such as canvas and zoom, which require an internet connection. This makes the mobile phone a device quite suited for the tasks, since it does not tie the user down to a specific physical place, as it can be used from anywhere. However, it does require that the device has service and cellular data such as 4G or 5G, or the use of internet in a café, or other hotspots.

The mobile phone was originally a technical device, but over time it has turned into a device more connected to the social aspect of life. Such as communicating with our friends and families over any distances within a short amount of time. However, this may also impact our ability to be social in real life. This gateway into social paradise, can also lead to isolation. For instance, if you don’t have the tools necessary, internet or a phone, or you decide to not be a part of the change this brings to society. This can for example be seen with elderly people that don’t follow the evolution of the technology around them, and get overly dependent on family, or other means of help, to function in society.

As a result, the mobile phone can be seen as one of our most important devices to our professional and social life.
